## 11. Seed Q&A Database (Pre-load these 8 Questions & Answers)

### Q1: What is included in the Spiti Valley tour package?
**A:** Our premium, all-inclusive tour packages include comfortable transport (4x4 SUVs or adventure-spec motorcycles), all overnight accommodations (premium hotels and traditional village homestays), delicious daily breakfast and dinner, all inner-line permits, professional local tour guides, and onboard safety equipment including medical-grade oxygen cylinders.

### Q2: What is the best time to plan a Spiti Valley trip?
**A:** The best season depends on the experience you are seeking. Summer (June to October) is ideal for warm weather, green meadows, and completing the full circuit via Manali. Winter (December to March) is perfect for deep snow landscapes, frozen water trails, and snow leopard safaris.

### Q3: How do we prevent Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) during the tour?
**A:** We prevent AMS by planning a gradual altitude ascent. Our tours enter via Shimla and climb slowly through Kinnaur, giving your body ample time to adjust to high altitudes. We also advise staying well-hydrated, avoiding immediate physical strain upon arrival in Kaza, and keeping oxygen cylinders on hand in all backup vehicles.

### Q4: Do Indian tourists need a permit for Chitkul and Spiti Valley?
**A:** Indian citizens do not need Inner Line Permits to travel through Kinnaur or Spiti Valley. However, you must carry a valid government-issued photo ID card (such as Aadhar Card, Driving License, or Passport) for mandatory registration at ITBP border check posts.

### Q5: Do you arrange motorcycle rentals for the Spiti bike trip?
**A:** Yes, we provide premium adventure-ready motorcycles (such as Royal Enfield Himalayan or Classic 350) equipped with luggage carriers and spares. All our bike trips are accompanied by an expert lead rider, a professional motorcycle mechanic, and a support utility vehicle carrying luggage and medical gear.

### Q6: Can families and children travel safely to Spiti Valley?
**A:** Yes, families can travel safely, provided the itinerary utilizes a gradual, clockwise route starting from Shimla to ensure slow altitude acclimatization. We recommend planning the journey during the pleasant summer months (June to September) for maximum comfort.

### Q7: What are the accommodation facilities in winter Spiti tours?
**A:** In deep winter (sub-zero temperatures), hotels in Kaza close due to frozen water pipes. Travelers are accommodated in traditional, insulated local homestays. These homestays feature wood-burning heaters (bukharis) in the living room and provide hot water in buckets. It offers a warm, deeply authentic cultural experience.

### Q8: Is mobile network connectivity available in Spiti Valley?
**A:** Mobile connectivity is highly limited. BSNL has broad coverage in Kaza, but high-speed data is absent. Recently, Airtel and Jio have introduced 4G services in Kaza, Sangla, and Kalpa, but remote regions and high passes have no active network signals.
